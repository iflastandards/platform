# Compound Title Of Parallel Title Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
This refers to a <strong>parallel title</strong> (element 1.2) that itself consists of a <strong>common title and a dependent title</strong>, serving as an equivalent in another language or script to a compound title proper.
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Parallel common title (Optional):</strong> The equivalent of the common title of the title proper in another language or script [1.2.5.5, 196].</li>
  <li><strong>Parallel dependent title (Optional):</strong> The equivalent of the dependent title of the title proper in another language or script [1.2.5.5, 196].</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>The <strong>entire parallel title statement</strong> (including its compound parts) is preceded by <strong>=</strong> within the Title and Statement of Responsibility Area (Area 1).</li>
  <li>The dependent part follows the common part, generally separated by a <strong>point (.)</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Relationship to ISBD Standards

<div className="guid">
This is a specific structure for element <strong>1.2 (Parallel title)</strong>, detailed in <strong>1.2.5.5</strong> of the ISBD. It is used when the main title is compound and its parallel equivalent is also structured in parts.
</div>

## Relationship to Other SES

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>It is a form within the "Title And Statement Of Responsibility Area Encoding Scheme."</li>
  <li>It applies the "Compound Title Of Title Proper Encoding Scheme" (1.1.3.7) to parallel titles.</li>
  <li>Changes to parallel titles for continuing resources may be noted in the <strong>Note Area (Area 7)</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
