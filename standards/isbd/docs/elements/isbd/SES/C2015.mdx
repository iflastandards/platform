# Specific Material Designation And Extent Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
This refers to the naming and numbering of the physical unit(s) that constitute the resource, along with other measures of its extent. It is the <strong>first element</strong> in the <strong>Material Description Area (Area 5)</strong> [411, 5.1]. The <strong>specific material designation (SMD)</strong> identifies the specific class of physical object.
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Number of physical units (Optional):</strong> Given in arabic numerals (e.g., "36"). This is omitted if the resource is still being issued or the number is unknown.</li>
  <li><strong>Specific material designation (Mandatory):</strong> A term indicating the type of physical unit (e.g., "slides," "atlas," "computer disk," "website"). Terms are not prescribed and may be abbreviated.</li>
  <li><strong>Subunits (Optional):</strong> The number and type of internal subunits (e.g., pages, leaves, frames, pieces) and/or playing time, enclosed in parentheses (e.g., "(37 p.)", "(ca 60 min)").</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>The <strong>subunits statement</strong> is enclosed in <strong>parentheses ( )</strong>.</li>
  <li>For multimedia resources, components are separated by <strong>,</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Relationship to ISBD Standards

<div className="guid">
This is element <strong>5.1</strong> within Area 5. It is fundamental to the physical description of a resource, providing a concise summary of its physical form and quantity.
</div>

## Relationship to Other SES

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>It is a component of the "Material Description Area Encoding Scheme."</li>
  <li>Playing time (5.1.5) and pagination (5.1.4) are detailed aspects of this element.</li>
  <li>It is closely related to the "Content Form And Media Type Area Encoding Scheme" (Area 0), as the physical carrier described here is the "Media type" in Area 0.</li>
  <li>For component parts, this information may be inferred from the host and location, and thus omitted from the part's description itself.</li>
  <li>Notes on extent (e.g., if numbering gives a false impression, or if it differs from bibliographic units) are given in the <strong>Note Area (Area 7)</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
