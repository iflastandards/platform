# Series And Multipart Monographic Resource Area Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
<p>This area (Area 6) is used to indicate that the resource being described belongs to a <strong>larger bibliographic resource</strong>, specifically a series or a multipart monographic resource. It helps to contextualize the individual resource within a broader collection.</p>
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Title proper of a series or multipart monographic resource (Mandatory if available):</strong> The collective title of the larger resource, transcribed as it would be if described as a serial or multipart monographic resource.</li>
  <li><strong>Parallel title of a series or multipart monographic resource (Optional):</strong> An equivalent series title in another language or script.</li>
  <li><strong>Other title information of a series or multipart monographic resource (Optional):</strong> Subordinate information qualifying the series title, given if necessary for identification or importance.</li>
  <li><strong>Statement of responsibility relating to a series or multipart monographic resource (Optional):</strong> Identifies persons or corporate bodies responsible for the series, especially if the title is generic, or if considered important.</li>
  <li><strong>International standard number of a series or multipart monographic resource (Mandatory if available):</strong> The ISBN or ISSN related to the series or multipart monographic resource (e.g., ISSN 0306-9222).</li>
  <li><strong>Numbering within a series or multipart monographic resource (Mandatory if available):</strong> The numbering of the specific resource within that series (e.g., "no. 78", "vol. 2").</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Each series statement</strong> is enclosed in <strong>parentheses ( )</strong>.</li>
  <li>A <strong>second and each subsequent series statement</strong> is preceded by a space.</li>
  <li>Each <strong>parallel title or parallel statement</strong> is preceded by <strong>=</strong>.</li>
  <li>Each <strong>statement of other title information</strong> is preceded by <strong>:</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>first statement of responsibility</strong> is preceded by <strong>/</strong>.</li>
  <li>Each <strong>subsequent statement of responsibility</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>international standard number</strong> is preceded by <strong>,</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Numbering within a series or multipart monographic resource</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>section or subseries designation or dependent title</strong> following a common title is preceded by <strong>.</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>dependent title</strong> following a section or subseries designation is preceded by <strong>,</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Relationship to ISBD Standards

<div className="guid">
<p>Identified as <strong>Area 6</strong>, this area helps to establish hierarchical relationships between bibliographic resources. It explicitly uses the concepts of "common title" and "dependent title" for series and subseries.</p>
</div>

## Relationship to Other SES

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>It defines how "Title Proper (Compound) Of Series Or Multipart Monographic Resource Encoding Scheme" and "Parallel Title (Compound) Of Series Or Multipart Monographic Resource Encoding Scheme" are applied.</li>
  <li>If a resource is part of a subseries with its own ISSN, the ISSN of the main series may be noted in the <strong>Note Area (Area 7)</strong>.</li>
  <li>Common titles of a main resource for a supplement or insert may be given in this area.</li>
  <li>Information on series editors can also be found in Area 7.</li>
</ul>
</div>
