# has longitude and latitude

The element "longitude and latitude" (or a direct equivalent) is not explicitly defined as a distinct element within the ISBD standard provided. Instead, longitude and latitude are components of the larger "Coordinates" element within the Mathematical data area (Area 3.1.3) for Cartographic resources.

## Element Reference

- **Definition (implied):** Longitude and latitude values provide geographic coordinate information delimiting the spatial extent of a cartographic resource. These are given as part of the Coordinates element which "delimit the greatest extent of the area covered and are recorded when present on the resource, or may be supplied if they are known and considered important to users of the catalog."
- **Domain:** Cartographic resources
- **Range:** String representing coordinates
- **Element Type:**  Component of a text string within the larger "Coordinates" element.


## Additional Information

- **Scope and Purpose:** To precisely define the geographic area represented by a map, chart, or other cartographic resource, aiding in discovery and retrieval.
- **User Tasks Supported:** Discovering resources covering a specific geographic area, determining the spatial coverage of a map, geospatial indexing and searching.


## Element Values

- **Rules and Guidelines:** Longitude and latitude are expressed in degrees (°), minutes ('), and seconds (") of the sexagesimal system (360° circle).  They can also be expressed as decimal degrees.  Values are preceded by W (West), E (East), N (North), and S (South) or their equivalents. Two sets of coordinates are separated by a slash (/), and each coordinate pair (e.g., west/east longitude) is linked by a hyphen (-). The coordinate system or meridian of reference (if not Greenwich) is specified after the coordinates, separated by a comma and space.
- **Value Constraints/Formats:** Follow the specific formatting described above.  Values must represent valid coordinates.
- **String Encoding:**  UTF-8 is recommended.

## Relationships

- **Relationship to other ISBD elements:** Longitude and latitude are part of the "Coordinates" element, which is, in turn, part of the "Mathematical data" area (3.1) along with "Statement of scale" and "Statement of projection."
- **Supertypes:** Coordinates
- **Subtypes:**  Longitude, Latitude (although not formally defined in ISBD as separate elements)
- **Inverse Relationships:** None specified within ISBD.


## Stipulations

- **Special Considerations:**  For celestial charts, Right Ascension and Declination are used instead of longitude and latitude.
- **Exceptions:**  If a resource is not drawn to scale, "Not drawn to scale" is used instead of coordinate information. If the scale is unknown, "Scale not given" is used.
- **Best Practices:**  Always use the coordinate system provided on the resource. If no coordinate system is provided, assume Greenwich unless there's evidence to the contrary. Supply missing coordinate information from other reliable sources whenever possible, enclosing supplied information in square brackets. If coordinates cannot be determined, omit them. Ensure consistency in formatting and use of abbreviations.
