## Definition

<div className="guid">
This refers to the combined statement of coordinates (terrestrial or celestial), celestial hemisphere, epoch, 
and equinox for <strong>cartographic resources</strong>. It is a crucial part of the <strong>Mathematical Data (Area 3.1)</strong>. 
While the query lists "Statement of Coordinates and Equinox", the ISBD sources show "Statement of coordinates and 
celestial hemisphere" (3.1.3) and "Statement of epoch and equinox" (3.1.4) as distinct elements, 
which are often grouped together in punctuation.
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Coordinates (Mandatory if applicable):</strong><ul>
      <li><strong>Terrestrial coordinates:</strong> Longitude (W/E) and latitude (N/S) given in degrees, minutes, seconds 
        (e.g., "E 79°–E 86°/N 20°–N 12°").</li>
      <li><strong>Right ascension and declination (Celestial):</strong> RA in hours, minutes, seconds and 
        Decl. in degrees, minutes, seconds (with + for northern, - for southern celestial hemisphere).</li>
      <li><strong>Other celestial coordinate systems:</strong> Decimal degrees for longitude and latitude, 
        with the name of the system (e.g., "ecliptic coordinates").</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li><strong>Celestial hemisphere (Optional):</strong> Specifies the northern, southern, 
    or both celestial hemispheres.</li>
  <li><strong>Epoch (Mandatory if available):</strong> An arbitrary moment in time to which measurements of 
    position are referred.</li>
  <li><strong>Equinox (Mandatory if available):</strong> One of two points of intersection of the ecliptic and 
    the celestial equator, occupied by the sun when its declination is 0°.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>The statements of <strong>coordinates, celestial hemisphere, epoch, and equinox</strong> are enclosed in 
    <strong>one pair of parentheses ( )</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>statement of celestial hemisphere</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>statement of equinox</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>Within coordinate ranges, individual values are linked by <strong>–</strong> (no space) and 
    different coordinate types by <strong>/</strong> (no space).</li>
</ul>
</div>
