# Music Format Statement Area Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
This is a sub-area within the <strong>Material or Type of Resource Specific Area (Area 3)</strong>, used specifically for <strong>notated music resources</strong>. It describes the <strong>physical form of presentation</strong> of the music (e.g., "Full score," "Chorus score," "Parts"). It is not used for works for solo instruments/voices or one voice and one accompanying instrument.
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Music format statement (Mandatory if available):</strong> The term or phrase for the physical presentation form, transcribed as it appears on the resource. Explanatory phrases may be included.</li>
  <li><strong>Parallel music format statement (Optional):</strong> An equivalent music format statement in another language or script.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>Each area is preceded by <strong>. —</strong>.</li>
  <li>Each <strong>parallel music format statement</strong> is preceded by <strong>=</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Relationship to ISBD Standards

<div className="guid">
This is element <strong>3.2</strong> within Area 3. It's a key part of the specialized description for notated music.
</div>

## Relationship to Other SES

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>It is a component of the "Material Or Type Of Resource Specific Area Encoding Scheme."</li>
  <li>Details of the physical units (e.g., number of pages, dimensions) are given in the <strong>Material Description Area (Area 5)</strong>.</li>
  <li>If the term "edition" in the resource indicates version or arrangement of music, it's given in Area 1; if it indicates the music format, it's given in this area.</li>
  <li>Notes on music format peculiarities are given in the <strong>Note Area (Area 7)</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>
