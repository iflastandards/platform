# Resource Identifier And Terms Of Availability Area Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
<p>This area (Area 8) includes identifiers (numbers or alphanumeric designations) that identify a resource 
according to international standards (e.g., ISBN, ISSN, ISMN) or publishers' designations. 
It also includes statements about the terms under which the resource is available (e.g., price, distribution conditions).</p>
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Resource identifier (Optional):</strong><ul>
      <li><strong>Standard identifiers:</strong> ISBN, ISSN, ISMN, URN, DOI, URL, preceded by their customary labels.</li>
      <li><strong>Publisher's number (Notated music only):</strong> Preceded by "Publ. no.".</li>
      <li><strong>Plate number (Notated music only):</strong> Preceded by "Pl. no.".</li>
      <li><strong>Label name and catalogue number (Sound/videorecordings):</strong> The label name followed by 
        all catalogue numbers.</li>
      <li><strong>Fingerprint (Older monographic resources):</strong> A unique alphanumeric string.</li>
      <li><strong>Music incipit (Music manuscripts):</strong> A series of notes from predetermined staves, 
        serving as an identifier.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li><strong>Qualification to identifier (Optional):</strong> A brief statement (in parentheses) that qualifies, 
    explains, or corrects the identifier (e.g., binding type, "(corrected)", "(invalid)").</li>
  <li><strong>Key title (Mandatory for continuing resources if ISSN is given):</strong> The unique name assigned by 
    the ISSN Network, inseparably linked to its ISSN.</li>
  <li><strong>Terms of availability (Optional):</strong> The price (with ISO 4217 currency code) or a statement of 
    other terms if not for sale (e.g., "for hire," "free to educational institutions").</li>
  <li><strong>Qualification to terms of availability (Optional):</strong> Qualifications to the terms, 
    enclosed in parentheses (e.g., "(to members)", "(annual subscription)").</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>Each area is preceded by <strong>. —</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>key title</strong> is preceded by <strong>=</strong>.</li>
  <li><strong>Terms of availability</strong> are preceded by <strong>:</strong>.</li>
  <li>A <strong>qualification</strong> is enclosed in <strong>parentheses ( )</strong>. 
    Multiple qualifiers are separated by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>Each <strong>subsequent identifier and terms of availability statement</strong> 
    (if repeating the area for multiple identifiers) is preceded by <strong>. —</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Relationship to ISBD Standards

<div className="guid">
<p>Identified as <strong>Area 8</strong>, this area provides crucial access and identification points for users, 
especially for purchasing or locating specific versions of a resource. 
The area can be repeated if a resource has multiple identifiers.</p>
</div>

## Relationship to Other SES

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>It contains the "Resource Identifier Statement Encoding Scheme" and 
    "Terms Of Availability Statement Encoding Scheme."</li>
  <li>Identifiers relating to bibliographic history (e.g., ISBNs for earlier editions) are typically not included here 
    but can be given in the <strong>Note Area (Area 7)</strong>.</li>
  <li>Physical details related to different formats might be recorded here if omitted from Area 5.</li>
  <li>The "Label name and catalogue number" here is distinct from publisher names in Area 4.</li>
</ul>
</div>
