# Series And Multipart Monographic Resource Area Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
This is a sub-area within the <strong>Material or Type of Resource Specific Area (Area 3)</strong> specifically for 
<strong>cartographic resources</strong>. It includes details like scale, projection, coordinates, celestial hemisphere, 
epoch, equinox, and magnitude, which are essential for describing maps, charts, and globes.
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Statement of scale (Mandatory if applicable):</strong> Given as a representative fraction (e.g., "1:25 000"). 
    Can include vertical scale for relief models or angular scale for celestial charts.</li>
  <li><strong>Statement of projection (Mandatory if available):</strong> The name of the projection 
    (e.g., "universal transverse Mercator proj."), possibly with associated phrases.</li>
  <li><strong>Statement of coordinates and celestial hemisphere (Mandatory if available):</strong> This covers:<ul>
      <li><strong>Terrestrial coordinates:</strong> Longitude and latitude in degrees, minutes, and seconds, 
        with cardinal directions.</li>
      <li><strong>Celestial coordinates (Right ascension and declination):</strong> RA in hours/minutes/seconds 
        and Decl. in degrees/minutes/seconds, with +/- for hemispheres.</li>
      <li><strong>Other celestial coordinate systems:</strong> Ecliptic, galactic, 
        supergalactic coordinates.</li>
      <li><strong>Celestial hemisphere:</strong> Indication of northern, southern, 
        or both hemispheres.</li>
    </ul>
  </li>
  <li><strong>Statement of epoch and equinox (Mandatory if available):</strong> A reference moment in time for position 
    measurements (epoch) and one of the intersection points of the ecliptic and celestial equator (equinox).</li>
  <li><strong>Statement of magnitude (Mandatory if available):</strong> For celestial charts, 
    the brightness magnitude (e.g., "limiting magnitude 3.5").</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>The <strong>projection statement</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>The statements of <strong>coordinates, celestial hemisphere, epoch and equinox</strong> are enclosed in 
    <strong>one pair of parentheses ( )</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>statement of equinox</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>statement of celestial hemisphere</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>The <strong>statement of magnitude</strong> is preceded by <strong>;</strong>.</li>
  <li>Within coordinates, pairs are separated by <strong>/</strong> (no space), 
    and ranges by <strong>–</strong> (no space).</li>
</ul>
</div>
