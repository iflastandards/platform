# Parallel Title (Compound) Of Series Or Multipart Monographic Resource Encoding Scheme

## Element Reference
<ElementReference frontMatter={frontMatter} />

## Definition

<div className="guid">
This refers to a <strong>parallel title</strong> for a series or multipart monographic resource that is also structured with a <strong>common title and a dependent title</strong>. It is an equivalent of a compound series title proper in another language or script [6.2.2, 482].
</div>

## Required Elements

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li><strong>Parallel common title (Optional):</strong> The equivalent of the series common title in another language or script [6.2.2].</li>
  <li><strong>Parallel dependent title (Optional):</strong> The equivalent of the series dependent title in another language or script [6.2.2].</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Punctuation Rules

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>The <strong>entire parallel title statement</strong> (including its compound parts) is preceded by <strong>=</strong>.</li>
  <li>The dependent part follows the common part, generally separated by a <strong>point (.)</strong> [484, example from 1.2.5.5].</li>
  <li>The <strong>entire series statement</strong> is enclosed in <strong>parentheses ( )</strong>.</li>
</ul>
</div>

## Relationship to ISBD Standards

<div className="guid">
This is element <strong>6.2.2</strong> within the Series and Multipart Monographic Resource Area (Area 6). It applies the compound title structure to parallel series titles.
</div>

## Relationship to Other SES

<div className="guid">
<ul>
  <li>It is a form within the "Series And Multipart Monographic Resource Area Encoding Scheme."</li>
  <li>It is analogous to the "Compound Title Of Parallel Title Encoding Scheme" (1.2.5.5) but applied to the series level.</li>
</ul>
</div>
